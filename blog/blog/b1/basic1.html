Kullback–Leibler(KL) divergence

${KL(p||q) = \int_x p(x) log{p(x) \over q(x)} dx }$

This is a measure that will tell you the difference between two probability distributions. Here p and q are two probability distributions. $KL(p||q)$ is zero when $p(x)=q(x)$ for all $x$.

  • $KL(p||q) \ge 0 $. [You can try to Prove]
  • More the value of $KL(p||q)$ more there is a difference between $p$ and $q$.
  • $KL(p||q) \ne KL(q||p)$. So these in not a symmetric measure

Transformation of Random Variables

To clear your concept please read this pdf.

  • In General If $X$ be a $n$ dimensional continuous random vector with density function $f(x)$. $Y$ be a $m$ dimensional continuous random vector and $Y=g(X)$. Then probability density function of Y is

    $f_Y(y)=f_X(g^{-1}(y))\times |J|$

    where $|J_{m \times n}|$ is the dererminant(iff $m=n$) of jacobian.

  • From the above comcept we get a nice formula of expectation which is very useful. let $x$ follows distribution $p_x$ and $y$ folows distribution $p_y$ and $g(x)=y$ then

    $E_{x \sim p_{x}}[g(x)]=E_{y \sim p_{y}}[y]$
